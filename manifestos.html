What I seek to Accomplish

The next Nairobi City County Integrated Development Plan (CIDP) must reflect the aspirations of Kileleshwa Ward.

1

Water is Life

Kileleshwa Ward is growing rapidly. Unfortunately water and sewer services are not keeping up! Despite the Nairobi rationing schedule many residents report either insufficient or no water on the appointed days.

My campaign pledges to ensure that Kileleshwa is properly represented under the Master Plan for Developing New Water Sources for Nairobi City and Satellite Towns. In the short term, I will fight for the right amount of rationed water on our scheduled days. That’s a promise!

Our sewer capacity requires proper planning & maintenance. Sewage along our roads and in our rivers is unhealthy & unsightly.

2

More than just a Road

Kileleshwa roads are more than just car routes. Our community needs high quality roads, pedestrian and cycle lanes.

Patching old roads is not enough. We deserves a long term plan for upgrading & maintaining roads to modern standards. This includes adequate drainage, signage and consistent street lighting.

As our Community expands we want to walk, run and cycle more. Our roads MUST BE SAFE. We will work with residents, business owners and other stakeholders, including boda bodas to respect road rules.

At a minimum, our Community expects the road standard achieved for the Kileleshwa Ring Road (also known as ‘Missing Link 6” or “bypass”). Where there is insufficient road reserve space the standard expected will be that achieved on Kikambala Road.

I will prioritize decent public transport, including appropriate bus / matatu pick up & rest areas that are clean and safe.

3

Women & Youth Empowerment

Kileleshwa Ward is no different from the rest of Kenya. The majority of residents are female. Additionally, youth (male and female under 35) are a rapidly growing demographic. Giving both these important groups extra attention is a recipe for future success. These individuals are our mothers, sisters, sons, daughters, nephews & nieces. Giving them a helping hand lifts the entire community.

Education - Public education in our Ward lags far behind private facilities. For all our youth to have a fair chance to succeed we need options. The CIDP post 2022 must help reinvigorate public schools like Kileleshwa Primary in the Ward through modern facilities & equipment support.

Employment – Young people need to develop livelihoods. The small businesses run by young residents and business owners will be supported by the Ward office through:

  1. Advocacy for affordable regulation (licences)
  2. Non-harassment of compliant businesses
  3. Formation of youth business groups
  4. Designate areas for various business to reduce conflict
4

A safe Kileleshwa for All

The safety of life, property and livelihoods in our Ward is non-negotiable. Every resident and business owner has the right to a safe environment. This begins with community engagement – we need to work together to identify threats and find solutions. I will work with the National Police Service representatives in the Ward to ensure regular neighbourhood patrols and quick responses to Community alerts.

However, security is a whole of Community function. If you “see something, say something”! The Kileleshwa Ward office will support a 24 hour online system to receive and escalate community security concerns to enhance existing security initiatives.

Together with the “More than Just a Road” initiative, I will work with security agencies for the full implementation of the street camera system along all major Ward roads.

5

A Clean, Green & Vibrant Community

It should not matter where you live or work in our Ward. From Waruku to Westlands, everybody deserves a clean and vibrant community.

Kileleshwa is blessed with rivers along our boundaries, including Chiromo & Kirichwa Kubwa Rivers. We need to work together with government to keep our rivers free of sewage and other debris. Modern thinking around river management is needed to avoid needless evictions and demolitions based on short term measures.

What happened to Kileleshwa community spaces? Where are children expected to play? We will collaborate with like-minded stakeholders to develop playgrounds and enhanced green spaces for community use.

Our community must address solid waste management. This starts from our homes and streets. I will ensure there is funding to install litter bins along our streets and ensure garbage is regularly collected..
